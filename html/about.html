Parque bosque encantado

Location: Between carrera 45 and calle 34A in Barrio Álvarez

About the park: metropolitan park "bosque encantado" it was created as a new space for natural recreation in cabecera it has more than 331 square meters where people can rest of the noises in the city enjoying the view from the high part of the city.
This park has a small library very comfortable for children of all ages, the library counts with Customes, musical instruments, puppets and color pencils for the children to enjoy outside their home with their guardians or parents, furthermore it has a sandbox and gym for the adults to enjoy as the children play in the library.
The park promotes the conservation of the environment having an urban garden that makes the community participate in the growing of the same. People can walk through the walking trails shaped like a zigzag with different stages marked by signs that explain what you will find next, with tall trees that makes the ambient feel full of life and playgrounds with various games like swings, slides and a rope mesh climbing.

Points of interest:

  • - Children library with many activities in different times of the day
  • - Near Recrear las Américas
  • - Rope mesh climbing for the children to enjoy
  • - Playgrounds
  • - It has an outside gym for the adults to enjoy the nature as they exercise

Parque Guillermo Zorzano (Parque san pio)

Location: Cra. 33 #45 Bucaramanga

About the park: This iconic park located in the heart of Cabecera, was inaugurated on July 20, 1964, in honor of the physician and former mayor of the city, Dr. Guillermo Zorzano Gonzales who was born in Piedecuesta in 1919 and died in 1960, this park was part of the Urbanas/Puyana developer, who donated their land as well as the 'Los Sarrapios' park. This park initially had water fountains, shrub areas, flat meadow areas, forest areas, selected plant species, tree-lined paths, and a sports area. However, the park had a large number of trees, which covered all the air space in the area, until a public works engineer cut the park in two, so a CAI was installed and the San Pio X church was relocated. With the arrival of the XXI Century, in 2011 it was remodeled and was also installed the sculpture of Fernando Botero, a Naked Standing Woman, becoming an attraction not only for the “bumangueses but also for the foreigners, giving greater relevance to the park.

Points of interest:

  • - St. Pio X Church
  • - Naked standing woman "la gorda de botero".
  • - San Pio Park sports fields
  • - Farmers' markets (Sundays)
  • - CLOBI
  • - Hotel Hampton by Hilton Bucaramanga
  • - McDonald's San Pío
  • - Outdoor gym.

Parque de los niños

Location: between 31st and 32nd streets and 26th and 27th avenues.

About the park: Information: The Children's Park, located between 31st and 32nd streets and 26th and 27th avenues, is a landmark in the city of Bucaramanga, although its registered name is that of the Comunero José Antonio Galán. This park was built during the first decade of the 20th century, and according to many historians, fairs, events, and popular neighborhood beauty pageants were held here in its early days.
The first parks in Bucaramanga were dedicated to honoring the memory of national and local heroes and martyrs, but the sculpture of José Antonio Galán was erected in the park much later. However, the park takes its name from the leader, and it was created out of the need to provide the city with a true natural forest that could serve as a site for walking and a healthy and beneficial place for the health of children and the elderly. General Eliseo Camacho had a five-block area called "Campo Camacho," which he donated to the Bucaramanga council. It was the council that designated that the work would be carried out by prisoners and detainees who requested commutation of their sentences for public works projects. Ambrosio and Enrique López drew up the initial plans for the park's foundations, and they themselves chose the species of plants and trees to be planted.

Points of interest:

  • - Sports courts
  • - Los Comuneros Hospital
  • - Bike rental from CLOBI
  • - Gabriel Turbay Public Library
  • - Our Lady of Fatima Church
  • - Statue of José Antonio Galán
  • - Monument to the 19 merchants who died in the massacre of Puerto Boyacá
  • - Statue of "El Clavijero de Tiple"
